## How I write

- Short sentences. Prefer one specific concrete thing per sentence over a general statement.
- Lead with what changed or what's needed. Skip filler openers ("just circling back", "wanted to follow up").
- Plain words. No corporate boilerplate.
- Tag teammates by @handle when they collaborated; don't credit yourself.

## Audience defaults

- **External partner replies (Zendesk, email):** warm but professional. Explain technical things in plain language. No internal jargon, no @handles.
- **Internal team posts (P2, Slack threads):** scannable. Bullets, fragments. Internal references and acronyms fine. Use @handles for collaborators.
- **Project decisions / briefs:** declarative. Lead with the decision; rationale on a second line.

## Anti-patterns

- "I just wanted to..." / "Quick question..." → drop the preamble.
- "Looking forward to hearing from you" → drop it.
- "Per my last email" → state the thing again instead.
- "Going forward" → say *when*.
